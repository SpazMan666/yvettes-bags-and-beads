Our Story

Bags Made Of Second Chances; Filled With Humble Beginnings with a Global Purpose

Yvette's Bags and Beads Collection has a powerful origin story that began in November 2007, born from a simple act of service. Its founder, Yvette Marie C. Punzalan, a computer engineer by profession, was asked by her father to teach crocheting at the Davao City Jail. There, she introduced a group of women deprived of liberty to an unconventional craft: creating with plastic twine from carton boxes. What started with twenty eager trainees tested their patience, and only ten remained. Yet, Yvette was so moved by their dedication that she was inspired to continue.

This endeavor transformed from a volunteer project into a profound mission when Yvette made a pivotal decision. She began compensating the women for their simple yet heartfelt bag designs. This act of payment became a catalyst for change, creating a tangible lifeline. She was so committed that she sought out the ten women who had initially left, determined to offer them the same opportunity. Witnessing the impact, how one artisan could now support her child's education and how families were becoming whole again as mothers reclaimed their treasured roles, Yvette knew this was her true calling. This conviction led her to formally register the business with the DTI in 2008.

Today, the enterprise operates on a lean, impactful model, but its true value was never in the profit margin. While the unique, eco friendly material makes each bag distinctive, it is the powerful human story of hope and second chances that is stitched into every fiber. Each product is an invaluable testament to human transformation. When you choose one of our creations, you are supporting a mission far greater than just a business. You are helping to weave a future of dignity and resilience.
